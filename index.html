Ancient Yew Digital Twin

A prototype 3D digital twin of an ancient yew tree in Surrey, created from Luma AI scans.

Newlands Corner yew grove

Newlands Corner is not just a scenic woodland site. The Ancient Yew Group records its First Grove as a public woodland site near Guildford, with 8 ancient yews in the 5-7m girth class, 12 ancient yews in the 4-5m girth class, and 4 notable yews below 4m girth.

This makes the site an unusually dense cluster of ancient and veteran yews, and a strong candidate for repeat 3D documentation.

Why yews?

Yew is one of Europe's longest-lived native tree species. Ancient yews often develop hollow trunks, complex branching structures, and irregular growth forms that make simple age estimation difficult. That is exactly why 3D reconstruction is interesting: instead of relying only on a single girth measurement, repeat scans could capture structure, crown form, hollowing, storm damage, and change over time.
